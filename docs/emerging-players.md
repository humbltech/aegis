# Emerging Players in AI Agent Security, LLM Security, and AI Governance

## Executive Summary

The AI security market has exploded in 2024-2026, with venture funding reaching record levels and major M&A activity reshaping the landscape. This report profiles the emerging startups and open-source projects that are innovating in AI agent security, LLM protection, and AI governance.

**Key Market Indicators (2025-2026):**
- Total cybersecurity VC investment: ~$119 billion in 2025
- AI/ML security startups raised $73.6 billion in Q1 2025 alone
- Disclosed M&A deal value: $96 billion across 400 transactions in 2025 (270% YoY increase)
- Top 10 agentic AI security startups raised a combined $3.6 billion

## Part 5: Gaps, Weaknesses, and Opportunities

### Current Market Gaps

1. **Agent-to-Agent Security**: Most solutions focus on human-to-LLM or LLM-to-tool. Agent-to-agent communication security is nascent.

2. **Multi-Agent Orchestration Security**: As agentic systems become more complex with multiple cooperating agents, security for orchestration layers is underdeveloped.

3. **Supply Chain for AI**: While MLSecOps addresses model security, the broader AI supply chain (training data, fine-tuning data, plugins, MCP servers) lacks comprehensive solutions.

4. **Real-time Compliance**: Most solutions are detective rather than preventive for compliance requirements.

5. **Cost-Effective SMB Solutions**: Most funding and products target enterprise; SMB and startup-friendly options are limited.

### Common Weaknesses Across Emerging Players

1. **Early Revenue Stage**: Many have impressive funding but limited ARR evidence
2. **Narrow Focus**: Some solve one problem well but lack platform breadth
3. **Israel Concentration**: Heavy concentration of founders from 8200; geographic risk
4. **Acquisition Risk**: Many are likely acquisition targets rather than standalone companies
5. **Open Source Sustainability**: Commercial models for OSS projects unclear

### Emerging Opportunities

1. **Sovereign AI Security**: Localized AI security for regulatory compliance (EU AI Act, etc.)
2. **AI Security for Embedded/Edge**: Security for AI in IoT, automotive, industrial settings
3. **AI Security Benchmarking**: Independent testing and certification services
4. **Insurance-Linked AI Security**: Products tied to cyber insurance requirements
5. **AI Security Training/Certification**: Educational services for the new discipline
