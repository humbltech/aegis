## Equity Factor Framework

Score each person 1-10 on each factor, then apply weights:

```
equity = (time_commitment × 0.40) 
       + (expertise_relevance × 0.25) 
       + (network_value × 0.15) 
       + (idea_origination × 0.10) 
       + (risk_taken × 0.10)
```

**Factor definitions:**
- **Time commitment**: Hours per week dedicated. 10+ hrs/week = 10, 5-10 hrs = 7, 2-5 hrs = 4, advisory = 2
- **Expertise relevance**: How directly does their expertise build Rind's moat? Security architect in MCP = 10; generalist director = 6; CISO (buyer perspective) = 7
- **Network value**: Quality of relationships for customers, investors, partners. CISO at Fortune 100 = 10; Director with enterprise network = 7; Technical founder = 4
- **Idea origination**: Who conceived the core idea? 10 = primary, 5 = co-conceived, 0 = neither
- **Risk taken**: Opportunity cost + financial risk. Full-time = 10; evenings/weekends while employed = 5; advisory-only = 2

## Standard Vesting Terms (Non-Negotiable)

All founders (including idea originator) receive:
- **4-year vesting schedule**
- **1-year cliff** — if someone leaves in year 1, they receive 0%
- **Monthly vesting** after cliff — 1/48 per month
- **Acceleration on acquisition** — single-trigger (acquisition = full vest) recommended at this stage

Advisors (if CISO is advisory):
- **2-year vesting**, no cliff
- **Monthly vesting** — 1/24 per month
- Standard advisor amount: 0.25-1% depending on engagement level

## Legal Documents Required Before Any Work Together

All of these must be signed BEFORE any team member writes code or makes commits:

1. **Co-Founder Agreement** — roles, decision rights (CEO has final say on product/GTM; CTO has final say on architecture), vesting terms, departure terms, IP ownership
2. **PIIA (Proprietary Information and Inventions Assignment)** — every contributor assigns IP to the parent company; signed by all founders, advisors, and contractors
3. **NDA** — mutual, covers Rind's technical architecture and strategic plans
4. **If CISO is advisory:** Formal Advisor Agreement with defined scope (introductions, feedback sessions) and defined time commitment

**Template sources:**
- YC Standard Documents (ycombinator.com/documents) — co-founder agreements
- SAFE Note template — for future fundraising
- NVCA model documents — for advisor agreements

## Director's "Idea Origination" Credit

The director coined the original idea. How to handle:
- +5% equity (per YC framework) — already incorporated in scenarios above
- Does NOT mean the director owns the IP — the company owns all IP, all founders assign it
- The idea credit is a one-time equity adjustment, not an ongoing veto right
- Once vesting starts, the idea premium is earned over time alongside everyone else

---

## CISO's Network Value — How to Capture It

The CISO's primary contribution is network + enterprise credibility. To make sure this is captured:
- Define specific deliverables in the advisor/co-founder agreement: "X customer introductions in year 1", "Y advisory board meetings per quarter"
- Vesting contingent on continued engagement (standard for advisors)
- If CISO goes inactive (leaves role, etc.), unvested equity is forfeited

---

## Decision Process

When commitments are defined:
1. Each person independently scores themselves on all 5 factors
2. Share scores with the group
3. Discuss differences
4. Apply the weighted formula
5. Round to clean percentages
6. Both parties agree or negotiate until consensus
7. Sign co-founder agreement immediately

Do not delay the agreement. Equity conversations get harder over time as the project gains value.
