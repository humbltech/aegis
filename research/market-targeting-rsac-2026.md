## Executive Summary

Initial SMB focus is too risky. Mid-market + funded startups is the right target:

| Segment | Budget Reality | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| **Micro SMB** (<50 emp) | No dedicated security budget | SKIP |
| **Small SMB** (50-250 emp) | $43K/year avg, price sensitive | SECONDARY |
| **Mid-market** (250-2000 emp) | $300K-5M/year security spend | PRIMARY |
| **Funded startups** (Series B+) | Have budget, need enterprise-ready | PRIMARY |

## Why Not Pure SMB?

### The Uncomfortable Truth

From [Cyber Defense Magazine](https://www.cyberdefensemagazine.com/the-state-of-smb-cybersecurity-in-2026-key-trends-and-predictions/) and [Techaisle](https://techaisle.com/blog/563-the-90-billion-defense-cybersecurity-investments-of-smbs-and-midmarket-firms-in-2024):

| SMB Segment | Dedicated Security Budget? | Actually Buys Tools? |
|-------------|---------------------------|---------------------|
| <50 employees | Only 28% have budget line | Mostly DIY/free tools |
| 50-250 employees | Yes, ~$43K/year avg | Yes, but price sensitive |
| 250-500 employees | Yes, ~$200K-500K/year | Yes, actively buying |

**Key insight:** *"SMBs are targeted with the same level of sophistication as Global 2000 companies, but operate with a fraction of the budget and staff."*

### SMB Buying Behavior Reality

- 63% increased cyber spending in 2025
- BUT 58% exceeded budgets (reactive, not planned)
- 71% use managed services (want to buy, not build)
- 84% seek external technology advisors

**Translation:** Small SMBs want help but can't afford dedicated tools. They go through MSPs.

## Why Mid-Market Is the Sweet Spot

### Budget Reality

From [UnderDefense](https://underdefense.com/blog/cybersecurity-budget-for-mid-market-firms/):

| Metric | Mid-Market Value |
|--------|-----------------|
| Security as % of IT spend | 10-12% (15-18% regulated) |
| Per-employee annual spend | $1,200-2,500 |
| MDR/SOC allocation | 40-45% of security budget |
| Company size | 250-2,000 employees |
| Revenue range | $50M-$500M |

### Breach Economics (Why They Actually Pay)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Direct breach cost | $4.8M |
| Total breach impact | ~$29M |
| Case study (Synnovis) | £32.7M loss (~7x annual profit) |

**This reframes security spending as loss prevention, not discretionary expense.**

### What Mid-Market Actually Buys

1. **Managed services** - 40-45% of budget (they want to buy, not build)
2. **Cloud & identity security** - 60%+ workloads outside perimeter
3. **Compliance automation** - DORA, NIS2, PCI DSS 5.0 expanding
4. **Breach protection** - Warranties and insurance verification

## Funded Startups: The Other Sweet Spot

### Why Series B+ Startups?

| Factor | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| **Budget** | $20M+ raised, can spend on security |
| **Urgency** | Selling to enterprise = security questionnaires |
| **Technical** | Engineering-led, appreciate good tools |
| **Speed** | Fast decisions, not 6-month sales cycles |
| **Reference** | Logo value for other customers |

### Common Pain Points

1. *"Enterprise customers keep asking about our AI security posture"*
2. *"We need SOC 2 Type II, and AI is a new audit area"*
3. *"Our agents have access to customer data, how do we control them?"*
4. *"We can't afford enterprise tools but need enterprise-grade security"*

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary: Mid-Market AI Adopters

```
Company Profile:
├── Revenue: $50M - $500M
├── Employees: 250 - 2,000
├── Industries: Finance, Healthcare, Tech, Manufacturing
├── AI Maturity: Building or deploying AI-powered features
├── Compliance: SOC 2, HIPAA, PCI, or preparing for EU AI Act
├── Pain: "We're using AI but security team doesn't know how to protect it"
└── Budget: $50K-200K/year for AI security

Key Indicators:
├── Has AI/ML team or using AI features in product
├── Compliance-driven (regulated industry or enterprise customers)
├── Recently had security incident or audit finding
└── Engineering team >20 people

Buyer Personas:
├── CISO / VP Security (budget holder, risk-focused)
├── Security Architect (technical evaluator, needs depth)
├── Platform/ML Engineering Lead (implementer, needs easy integration)
└── Compliance Manager (influencer, needs audit evidence)
```

### Secondary: AI-First Startups

```
Company Profile:
├── Funding: Series B+ ($20M+ raised)
├── Employees: 50 - 500
├── Product: AI-native or heavy AI integration
├── Sales Motion: Selling to enterprise (security questionnaires)
├── Pain: "Enterprise customers ask about AI security, we have no answer"
└── Budget: $20K-100K/year for security

Key Indicators:
├── AI is core to product (not just chatbot)
├── Enterprise sales motion (not PLG only)
├── Recently closed enterprise deal or in procurement
└── CTO/VP Eng active on security

Buyer Personas:
├── CTO / VP Engineering (decision maker)
├── Security Lead (if exists, evaluator)
├── Head of Compliance (SOC 2 focused)
└── Solutions Engineer (deals with security questionnaires)
```

### Skip: Pure SMB

```
NOT targeting:
├── Companies <50 employees (no budget)
├── Non-tech SMBs (not using AI agents)
├── Companies without compliance requirements
├── Bootstrapped startups (price too sensitive)
└── MSP channel initially (complex GTM)
```

## Go-to-Market Implications

### Pricing Must Match Segment

| Tier | Price | Target | Rationale |
|------|-------|--------|-----------|
| **Starter** | $499/mo | Series A-B, small teams | Below enterprise, above free |
| **Growth** | $1,999/mo | Series B+, mid-market entry | Sweet spot for 100-500 emp |
| **Business** | $4,999/mo | Mid-market core | $60K ACV, justifiable for $50M+ co |
| **Enterprise** | Custom ($15K+/mo) | Large mid-market | Complex needs, custom SLAs |

### Sales Motion

**NOT doing:**
- Free tier with massive scale (SMB magnet, support burden)
- MSP/channel partner first (complex, slow)
- Enterprise (6-12 month sales cycles)

**Doing:**
- Developer-focused content marketing
- Free trial (14 days, credit card required)
- Product-led growth with sales assist
- Target ACV: $24K-60K (manageable for mid-market)

## Self-Hosted Models: The Hidden Opportunity

### Why This Matters for Mid-Market

From [AccuKnox](https://accuknox.com/blog/securing-ai-serving-engines-vllm-ollama-triton):

> *"vLLM is optimized for performance, not security; it requires external runtime protection."*

Mid-market and funded startups are increasingly self-hosting LLMs for:
- Data privacy (no data leaves their environment)
- Cost control (avoid API costs at scale)
- Latency (on-prem inference)
- Compliance (data residency requirements)

### Integration Approach

```
Phase 1 (MVP): Cloud API Models
├── OpenAI, Anthropic, Azure OpenAI
├── Simple proxy integration
└── 80% of market

Phase 2: Self-Hosted Support
├── Ollama (most popular, OpenAI-compatible API)
├── vLLM (enterprise performance)
├── Kubernetes Helm chart deployment
└── Gateway deploys in their cluster
```

**NOT building:**
- Full proxy (Zscaler's business)
- Browser extension (complex, limited coverage)
- Desktop agent (12-18 month effort)
